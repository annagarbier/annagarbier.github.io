Cardtops is a set of Augmented Reality mini-games built on top of public transportation cards. This page documents the early development of the games, and their future direction.

Why public transportation cards as AR targets? 1) widely accessible 2) situated as part of a distinct local culture and experience 3) available in times of boredom 4) technically easy for machine to recognize which allows for more freedom when deisgning interactions

Playing with card interactions: tapping, flipping, bending, and covering the AR target. Thinking about user considerations: e.g. Should it be a one-handed interaction? How much space will they have to move?. As well as technical considerations: e.g. What kind of target interaction can Unity & Vuforia reliably understand?

Ultimately chose to use the card as a platform that exists in 3d space. As the user moves this platform, a mini game plays out accordingly.

Designing the game, which will sit atop the card target. The game is designed to be short so that it can be played while waiting for a train.

Testing the maze game on a NYC Metro card. (See C# script here.)

Exploring the future of these games as not just one standalone game, but a system of games. Here, we have a new card, which triggers a new game. (See C# scripts here and here.)

Future Iterations
I'd like to build a "system" of mini-games that plays off of transportation as a system. Maybe these games engage with local attractions/businesses, or maybe they lead to collectable "tokens" as you use different cities' transportation systems.
