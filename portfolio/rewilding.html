Rewilding

In 2007, fifty or so nature-related words were removed from the Oxford Junior Dictionary, including acorn, bluebell, kingfisher, lark, and mistletoe. Among the words added were blog, broadband, chatroom, MP3 player, and voicemail.

Several writers have, in the years since, responded to this particular event through essays and wonderfully dense glossaries of the natural world. Rewilding joins those voices as a set of procedurally-generated botanical drawings that celebrate our vocabulary (both linguistic and visual) for nature: from the poetic to the strange to the precise.

Each imagined plant name was randomly generated based a glossary of common plant names (both extinct and living). The drawings then followed. These are, in Robert Macfarlane's words, a small attempt at "rewilding" our vocabulary-- and with it our imaginations.
