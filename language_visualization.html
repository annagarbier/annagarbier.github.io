Synesthetic Cryptograms

“Synesthetic Cryptograms” explore the hidden patterns of speech by translating sounds into visual maps.

In the spirit of cryptogram puzzles that I enjoyed throughout my childhood, this series takes units of speech sounds (phonemes) and shows them as blocks of colors based on the sounds’ features. The top image shows the place of articulation for a stream of speech: units of sound that are articulated at the front of the vocal tract are shown as blocks of light colors, sounds at the back as dark colors. The lower image shows airflow patterns for the same stream of speech: the release of air via sonorants is represented in blocks of cool colors, punctuated by obstructions to airflow in warm colors.

Source data is generated with the CMU Pronouncing Dictionary, an original Python script, and-- in this particular case-- the prologue to “Romeo and Juliet”.
